Understanding Date Calculations

Date calculations can vary based on different calendar systems and leap year considerations. Here are some key points to remember:

  • Leap Years: Years divisible by 4 are leap years, except for century years which must be divisible by 400
  • Month Lengths: Months have 28-31 days (April, June, September, November have 30; February has 28 or 29)
  • Date Formats: Our calculator uses ISO 8601 format (YYYY-MM-DD)
  • Week Calculation: 1 week = 7 days, but months have approximately 4.35 weeks

Date Calculation Tips

Consider these tips for accurate date calculations:

  • When adding months, the day may adjust if the target month has fewer days
  • Subtracting time works the same as adding negative time
  • For business days calculations, remember to exclude weekends and holidays
  • Date differences can be calculated in multiple formats (days, weeks, months, years)
  • Time zones can affect date calculations - all calculations are in UTC

Common Date Calculations

Some frequently used date calculations:

  • 90 days from today: Common for visa processing deadlines
  • 6 weeks from a date: Often used in pregnancy calculations
  • 18 years from birth: Legal adulthood in many countries
  • 10 business days: Common timeframe for document processing
  • Countdown to New Year: Popular celebration timer
