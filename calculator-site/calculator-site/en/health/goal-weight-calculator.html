Understanding Healthy Weight Goals

Achieving your goal weight requires a sustainable approach that combines proper nutrition, physical activity, and realistic expectations. This calculator helps you create a personalized plan based on scientifically-backed principles.

Key Principles for Success

Research shows that successful weight management involves:

  • Gradual changes: Aim for 0.5-1kg (1-2lb) loss/gain per week
  • Calorie balance: Create a moderate deficit/surplus of 500-1000 calories/day
  • Macronutrient balance: Adequate protein to preserve muscle mass
  • Consistency: Sustainable habits over time yield the best results

How the Calculation Works

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to determine your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), then factors in your activity level to calculate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation:

  • Male: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) + 5
  • Female: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) - 161

Activity Multipliers

Your TDEE is calculated by multiplying your BMR by an activity factor:

Activity Level Multiplier Description
Sedentary 1.2 Little to no exercise, desk job
Lightly Active 1.375 Light exercise 1-3 days/week
Moderately Active 1.55 Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week
Very Active 1.725 Hard exercise 6-7 days/week
Super Active 1.9 Physical job or training twice daily

Healthy Weight Change Rates

For sustainable results, follow these guidelines:

Goal Recommended Rate Calorie Adjustment
Weight Loss 0.5-1 kg/week 500-1000 calorie deficit
Weight Gain 0.25-0.5 kg/week 250-500 calorie surplus

Success Tips

Remember that weight change isn't linear. Plateaus are normal - focus on long-term trends rather than daily fluctuations. Combine your nutrition plan with regular physical activity for optimal health results.
